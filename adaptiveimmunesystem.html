Adaptive Immune System

The Adaptive Immune System, also referred as the Acquired Immune System.

It is a subsystem of the Immune System that is composed of cells that eliminates

Pathogens (Germs) by preventing their growth. Acquired immunity creates immunological

memory (is the ability of the Immune System to recognize an Antigen) the response

is immidately when encounter that Pathogen.It is important to Vaccinate (helps the immune

system develop protection from a disease).

The Major Functions of The Acquired Immune System include:

  • Recognize antigens during the process of antigen presentation (a vital immune process for T-cells immune response).
  • Response to eliminate specific Pathogens or Pathogen infected cells.
  • Development of Immunological memory (recognize an antigen encountered for immune response), in which Pathogens are "remembered" through Memory B cells(infection) and Memory T cells (a subset of infection and cancer fighting).
