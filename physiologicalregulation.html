Physiological Regulations

The Immune System is involved in many aspects of Physiological Regulations in the body.

The Immune System interacts intimately with other systems, such as Endocrine (a chemical

messenger system of hormones released by internal glands, major endocrine glands are the Thyroid

glands) and the nervous system (a highly complex part of a living being that coordinates its actions and

sensory information by signals to/from different parts of the body. The nervous system detects environmental

changes that impact the body). It plays a crucial role in Embryogenesis (a process by which

the embryo forms and develop), as well as in the tissue (a cellular organizational level between

cells and complete organ) repair and regeneration (a process of restoration).

Some of the Physiological Regulations and Definitions are:

  • Hormones
  • Vitamin D
  • Sleep and Rest
  • Nutrition and Diet
  • Repair and Regeneration

Hormones can act as immunomodulators (activate or supress the immune system), altering

the sensitivity of the immune system. On female sex hormones are known immunostimulators of both

adaptive and innate immune responses. On male sex hormones (testosterone is an anabolic steroid)

seem to be immunosuppressive.

When T-Cell encounters a Pathogen, it extends a Vitamin D receptor.

It gives a signal that allows a T-Cell to bind an active form of Vitamin D,

the steroid hormone Calcitriol (an active form of Vitamin D, normally made in the kidney)

by binding T-Cells perform their fuction. It is recommended to supplement your body

with Vitamin D hormones level in the human body decline with age.

The Immune System is affected by sleep and rest, insufficient sleep is detrimental to immune

functions. When suffering from sleep deprivation, creates lower antibody product, and lower

immune response. A disruption of insufficient sleep can lead to a chronic conditions such as

heart disease, chronic pain, and asthma. If you rest well your immune system will be more

responsive against any intruding pathogens.

Overnutrition is associated with diseases such as diabetes and obesity, which are known to affect

immune function. Malnutrition, as well as certain specific trace mineral and nutrients deficiencies

can compromise the immune response. Food rich in fatty acids may foster a healthy immune system.

The immune syste, particularly the innate component, plays a decisive role in tissue repair.

Key factors include Macrophages (a type white cell that digest cellular debris, foreign

substances, microbes, cancer cells) and Neutrophis (white blood cells in most mammals an

essential part of the immune system). The immune cells and the balance between pro-inflammatory

are crucial aspects of efficient tissue repair.
